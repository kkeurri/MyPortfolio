Artchive

ArtChive is a digital solution designed to help artists efficiently manage and organize their growing collections. By offering a user-friendly platform to document essential details, categorize works, and track productivity, it empowers artists, students, and educators to focus more on creativity and less on administrative tasks. Supporting Sustainable Development Goal 4, ArtChive promotes lifelong learning and artistic growth through better organization, accessibility, and peace of mind.

The goal of this project is to: Give artists the freedom to create without the fear of losing their work or progress.

WHAC'M Introducing Self-healing Process for Enhancement of Plastic-incorporated Concrete Hollow Block Using Bacillus Subtilis As A Self-healing Agent
WHAC'M addresses two environmental concerns: air and water pollution. With rising vehicle numbers contributing to increased emissions, and invasive water hyacinths causing waterway blockages, the researchers developed a filter for diesel vehicle emissions using activated carbon derived from water hyacinth. Characterization through FTIR and SEM confirmed the material's porous nature and comparability to commercial activated carbon. The filter effectively reduced emissions, proving to be a viable eco-friendly alternative. This study explores the use of PET plastic waste and encapsulated Bacillus subtilis to enhance the strength and sustainability of concrete hollow blocks (CHB). PET particles were processed from plastic bottles, while Bacillus subtilis was encapsulated in expanded clay with protective coatings. Six CHB samples were created and tested for compressive strength, density, and self-healing ability. Results showed that PET-incorporated CHBs with bacteria achieved comparable or improved performance, especially after 12 and 28 days.
